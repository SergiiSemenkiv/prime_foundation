PRIME MINISTER’S ADMINISTRATION

His Highness Sheikh / Sabah Khaled Al-Hamad Al-Sabah

PRIME MINISTER

Every government employee we consider to be part of the Prime Minister's team has roles that must be performed in the required manner and in an optimal manner. These roles are implemented under the supervision of the ministers.

Born in Kuwait on March 15, 1953, the son of Sheikh Khaled Hamad Al-Mubarak Al-Sabah and his mother, Sheikha Mozah Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah. He has 8 brothers and sisters. In 1977, he obtained a BA in Political Science from Kuwait University, and in 1978 joined the Ministry of Foreign Affairs with the rank of Diplomatic Attaché in the Arab Affairs Department of the Political Department from 1978 to 1983. He worked with the permanent delegation of the State of Kuwait to the United Nations in New York between 1983 and 1989.

In 1989, he was appointed as Deputy Director of the Arab World Department at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. In 1992, he was appointed Director of the Department of the Undersecretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. In 1995, he was appointed Kuwait's ambassador to the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and its delegate to the Organization of the Islamic Conference, a position he held until 1998, and by virtue of his position, he participated in the meetings of the Ministerial Council of Foreign Ministers of the Gulf Cooperation Council countries. In 1998, a decree was issued appointing him head of the National Security Apparatus with the rank of minister

On July 10, 2006, he was appointed Minister of Social Affairs and Labor, and on March 25, 2007, he was reappointed to the same position On October 28, 2007, he was appointed Minister of Information, and on May 28, 2008, he was reappointed to the same position

He was reappointed to the same position on January 12, 2009, and on April 6, 2009, he was appointed - in addition to his work - as Minister of Justice and Minister of Awqaf and Islamic Affairs, after its minister resigned due to his desire to run for the National Assembly elections and remained at the head of the three ministries until May 29, 2009, when the new government was formed, from which he left In 2010, he was appointed as a member of the Supreme Petroleum Council. On October 23, 2011, he was appointed Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs On December 13, 2011, he was appointed Deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Foreign Affairs and Minister of State for Cabinet Affairs[10], and on February 14, 2012, he was reappointed to the same position. On July 19, 2012, he was appointed as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, and on December 11, 2012, he was reappointed to the same position, and on August 4, 2013, he was reappointed to the same position.

On January 5, 2014, he was appointed as First Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs On November 19, 2019, he was appointed Prime Minister
